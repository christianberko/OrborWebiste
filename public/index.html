Our Process

OUR PROCESS

Collect

We partner with tennis facilities and players to collect used polyester tennis strings, ensuring they are diverted from landfills and repurposed for recycling into high-quality materials.

Repurpose

Our advanced recycling technology breaks down the polyester strings and transforms them into virgin-quality materials ready for manufacturing sustainable clothing.

Collaborate

The recycled materials are crafted into high-quality, sustainable fashion items, completing the circular economy and giving tennis strings a second life.

The Orbor Difference

Orbor is pioneering a new era of circularity in sports and fashion—turning tennis string waste into high-quality, traceable materials that power a cleaner, more transparent future.

Environmental Impact

Toxic Emissions

Incinerating tennis strings contributes to climate change by releasing approximately 2.5 kg of CO₂ per kg of polyester, along with toxic pollutants, into the atmosphere.

Carbon Footprint

Reduce CO₂ emissions from virgin polyester by 50%, helping to combat climate change and its impacts on weather, sea levels, ecosystems, and global livelihoods.

Micro Plastics

Prevent billions of microplastics from tennis strings entering our waterways, damaging marine ecosystems and entering the food chain, where they pose a threat to biodiversity and human health.

Who Are We?

Orobor, founded by tennis players at Boston University, is dedicated to revolutionizing recycling in the tennis and textile industries by transforming used tennis strings into high-purity polyester suitable for clothing manufacturing. Through innovation and collaboration, we aim to reduce waste, advance the circular economy, and inspire sustainable practices across industries—all while staying rooted in the values of teamwork and dedication we've learned on the court.
